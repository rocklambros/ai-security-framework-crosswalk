\begin{abstract}
Organizations adopting AI systems must comply with a growing number of
overlapping security frameworks, yet no shared vocabulary exists across
them. Manual crosswalks are expensive, error-prone, and quickly become
stale as frameworks evolve. We present an automated approach for mapping
controls across 12 major AI security frameworks, constructing a
knowledge graph of 983 control nodes and 6{,}383 edges. Our method
combines text embeddings (BGE-large, BM25) with structural features
from a GATv2 graph attention network, feeding a LightGBM stacker that
classifies control pairs into four tiers: \emph{equivalent},
\emph{related}, \emph{partial}, and \emph{unrelated}. Mondrian
conformal prediction provides per-class coverage guarantees, and a
KL-divergence router flags uncertain predictions for human review.
On 400 expert-labeled frozen test pairs the system achieves a tier
accuracy of 0.37 (95\% CI [0.33, 0.42]) and macro F1 of 0.23; on
1{,}515 LLM-labeled validation pairs it reaches 0.65 accuracy.
Ablation analysis confirms that GATv2 features are critical, lifting
accuracy by 10.8 percentage points over the text-only baseline. All
code, data splits, and model weights are released under Apache-2.0
with a one-command reproducibility target.
\end{abstract}
