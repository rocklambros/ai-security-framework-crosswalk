\begin{abstract}
Organizations adopting AI systems must comply with a growing number of
overlapping security frameworks, yet no shared vocabulary exists across
them. Manual crosswalks are expensive, error-prone, and quickly become
stale as frameworks evolve. We present an automated approach for mapping
controls across nine major AI security and governance frameworks,
constructing a heterogeneous knowledge graph of 983 control nodes
integrated from four complementary data sources: within-framework
hierarchies, community-contributed upstream mappings, cross-reference
tables, and bootstrap-pruned anchor pairs. Our method fine-tunes three transformer architectures
(RoBERTa-large, DeBERTa-base, BGE-large-v1.5) with ordinal-aware loss
functions, combining their predictions via softmax averaging to classify
control pairs into four tiers: \emph{equivalent}, \emph{related},
\emph{partial}, and \emph{unrelated}. Mondrian conformal prediction
provides per-class coverage guarantees. On 179 expert-labeled
frozen test pairs the system achieves tier accuracy of 0.799 (95\% CI
[0.737, 0.860]), macro F1 of 0.558 (95\% CI [0.436, 0.661]), and
adjacent accuracy of 0.922. Ablation analysis confirms that the
ensemble of diverse architectures and loss functions provides robust
probability estimates, with the ordinal losses enabling detection of
the rare Equivalent class (F1 = 0.400, up from 0.000 in v7c). All code, data splits, and model weights are released under
Apache-2.0 with a one-command reproducibility target.
\end{abstract}
