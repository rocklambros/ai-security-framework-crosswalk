\section{Limitations}
\label{sec:limitations}

\paragraph{Expert--LLM label gap.}
The most significant limitation is the 28-point accuracy gap between
LLM-labeled validation (0.65) and expert-labeled test (0.37). This
indicates that the LLM-SME labeling protocol produces systematically
different judgments from human experts, particularly for partial-overlap
and related tiers where the distinction is inherently subjective.

\paragraph{Class imbalance.}
The equivalent class (tier~3) has 0\% recall on the frozen test set.
The model never predicts this class with sufficient confidence, as
equivalent pairs are rare ($<$5\% of the dataset) and the LightGBM
stacker is biased toward the majority class despite inverse-frequency
weighting.

\paragraph{Conformal coverage gap.}
Empirical conformal coverage (0.69) falls short of the nominal 0.90
target. With only 400 test instances and four classes, the calibration
set is too small to achieve reliable per-class coverage for minority
tiers.

\paragraph{Framework coverage.}
While 12 frameworks are included, the system has not been evaluated on
frameworks outside this set. Generalization to new frameworks would
require additional prior edges or zero-shot graph construction methods.

\paragraph{Temporal validity.}
Framework versions are pinned at ingestion time. As frameworks evolve,
the crosswalk will require periodic re-ingestion and re-evaluation.
