\section{Results}
\label{sec:results}

\subsection{Main Results}

On the 400 expert-labeled frozen test set, our full system achieves:
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Tier accuracy:} 0.3725 (95\% CI [0.3275, 0.4200])
    \item \textbf{Macro F1:} 0.2259
\end{itemize}

On the 379 LLM-labeled validation set, accuracy rises to 0.6517,
reflecting the distribution gap between expert and LLM labeling
discussed in Section~\ref{sec:limitations}.

\subsection{Conformal Coverage}

The Mondrian conformal predictor achieves empirical coverage of 0.69
at $\alpha=0.10$, below the nominal 0.90 target. This shortfall is
attributable to the small calibration set and severe class imbalance
in the minority tiers. Coverage on the majority (unrelated) class
meets the nominal level.

\subsection{Router Statistics}

The KL-divergence router flags approximately 35\% of test predictions
for human review. Among routed instances, the error rate is 1.8$\times$
higher than among accepted instances, confirming that the router
successfully identifies uncertain predictions.
